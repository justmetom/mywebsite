1.2: The Methods, Skills and Resources needed to complete Digital Projects successfully

A successful project starts with planning. What are you trying to achieve, how are you going to go about it. What do you need? Use something like Trello to keep track and on schedule. Research your project subject, in this case look at how websites/blogs are created and used. Resources are what you will need for your project and include such things as people, equipment, space and funding. The skills needed to successfully complete the project will include the ability to work well with others, as well as on your own. Good communication is vital. While the ability to stay focused on the task at hand is important, flexibility is needed to be able to deal with any problems should they arise, and they usually do however well you have planned.

1.3: Planning and Carrying out Tasks using IT

Planning is key to successful completion of any project no matter what its size. Use a system like Kanban and a tool such as Trello to keep track of what needs doing, what is being done and what is complete. Also, of who in your team (if you have one) is doing what. A flow chart can be useful in the initial stages to sketch the steps needed to get to your desired outcome.

1.4: What are the Risks that might impact Digital Projects

Large projects might have more risks attached than small ones, such as having to deal with multiple outside contractors or stakeholders, but some risks are common to all projects. The biggest risk that might impact on your digital project is lack of planning. Not all risks can be planned for, but project risks should be a part of your initial planning as much as possible. Examples of risks that can be planned for include; - schedule risk, bottlenecks that can hold up the entire project - resource risk, lack of skilled staff for example or proor equipment - budget risk, over runs or budget being cut Things that might impact your project but be hard to plan for might include; - war - natural disasters - changes in legal requirements - even a little thing like the wi-fi being down can throw you ofF schedule

1.5: Selecting and Using IT Systems and Software

Selecting the right software and tools to use for your project is an important part of the planning process. If starting from scratch I would look at first the cost - is the return worth the investment?
