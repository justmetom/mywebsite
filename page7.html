Unit 1 outcome 3

3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency

I found Trello helpful in planning my project, but given the time restraints on the project, and my slow typing skills, I found I spent almost as much time updating Trello as I did on the actual writing and coding. While I hesitate to blame my tools, the borrowed laptop I used for this project was very old and slow. Faster equipment would have made a difference, as would me having more experience using basic computer skills. While it is nice to wholly own what you create, working in a team does have some advantages. It is good to have other people to bounce ideas off or to be able to ask for help with something.

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency

Technical issues aside, there are other ways to improve productivity and efficiency. Taking regular breaks away from your screen is helpful. It gives your eyes a rest and can also stop you getting stuck on one little thing, especially if it is not working well. Keeping fed and hydrated are important as is building a little physical activity, such as the yoga sessions a Code Nation into your work routine.

3.3 Develop solutions to improve own productivity using IT in digital projects

The most important thing I could do to increase my productivity is to practice my computer skills (typing etc.) and to practice coding. A faster computer, less prone to go-slow would improve productivity. While working in a team can involve more talking as well as doing, I think having people to bounce ideas off and to ask for help if you need it can lead to better productivity. There is less chance of one small issue holding up the whole project if there are others you can call on for help.

3.4 Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions

It can be hard, once a project you have been immersed in is finally complete, to always spot any mistakes that might have occurred. There is a tendency to just see what you expect to see. For this reason it is always a good idea to get someone else to go over your final work. They are more able to spot mistakes and give constructive criticism.
