1.5 Copyright and other Constraints affecting Websites

Copyright gives the holder exclusive rights to their original work. Any unautherised use of this work (it can be images, designs or text) is an infringement. Website designers need to be careful when using content from elsewhere on the internet, it is not always obvious what is free to use and what is protected. GDPR affects the collection, storing and using of personal data. Equality laws regulate the accessabilty of websites.

Unit 2, 1.6 Access issues that need taking into account

It is in the interest of website designers that their websites should be accessable to everyone. This means including such assistive technologies as screen magnifiers, screen readers or speech recognition tools in your build. Look at the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0 (WCAG20) issued by the Web Accesiblity Initiative (WAI).
